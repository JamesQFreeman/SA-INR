Efficient Arbitrary Reduction of MRI Slice Spacing

Xin Wang✝,  Sheng Wang✝,  Kai Xuan,  Qian Wang*

Paper Code

MR images collected in 2D protocols typically have large inter-slice spacing, resulting in high in-plane resolution and reduced through-plane resolution because of the limitations of scanning duration and signal-to-noise ratio. Super-resolution is a powerful tool to reduce the inter-slice spacing of MR images to facilitate subsequent visualization and computation tasks. However, most existing works train the super-resolution network at a fixed ratio, which is inconvenient in clinical scenes due to the heterogeneous parameters in MR scanning. In this paper, we propose a single super-resolution network for continuous reduction of MR slice spacing. By view the input image as a continuous implicit function of coordinates, the intermediate slices of different spacing ratios could be constructed according to the up-sampled coordinates in the continuous domain. Particularly, we propose a novel local-aware spatial attention operation to extend the reception field for each spatial coordinate, so as to boost the quality of output image. Meanwhile, we propose a gradient-guided gating mask for conditionally applying the local-aware spatial attention in the spatial domain. In this way, we could achieve comparable and even better performance while reducing the computation brought by the attention operation. The experimental results demonstrate the superiority of our proposed method, even compared to the models trained at a fixed ratio.

Self-Supervised Arbitrary Slice Spacing Reduction of Knee MRI Slice Spacing

In clinical practice, some body parts (e.g., knee and ankle) typically only requires 2D protocols acquired LR MR images because of the shorter scan time. Therefore, paired LR/HR images are not popularly available to offer a full voxel-to-voxel supervision. We deploy our proposed model on a self-supervised reduction of MRI slice spacing framework to test our model's ability to apply to real-world clinical scenarios.
